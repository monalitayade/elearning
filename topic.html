Introduction to Fractions
Course Overview

Photosynthesis is the process where plants, algae, and some bacteria convert light energy into chemical energy (sugars) using carbon dioxide and water, releasing oxygen as a byproduct.

The process:
  • Light-dependent reactions: Light energy is absorbed by chlorophyll and used to split water molecules, releasing oxygen and generating energy carriers (ATP and NADPH).
  • Light-independent reactions (Calvin cycle): The energy carriers from the light-dependent reactions are used to convert carbon dioxide into sugars.
Course Overview

What it is: Photosynthesis is a vital biological process that sustains life on Earth by converting light energy (typically from sunlight) into chemical energy in the form of sugars (like glucose).
